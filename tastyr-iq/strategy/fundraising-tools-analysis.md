## TIER 1 — Use Before Approaching Investors

### YC SAFE Documents
- **What**: Free, standardized Simple Agreement for Future Equity templates — the industry standard
- **Action**: Download post-money SAFE from ycombinator.com/documents
- **Cost**: Free

### Carta (free tier)
- **What**: Cap table management, equity planning, SAFE conversion modeling
- **Action**: Set up cap table with both cofounders' equity splits. Model SAFE conversion scenarios at different valuation caps ($5M, $8M, $10M)
- **Cost**: Free for up to 25 stakeholders

### Foresight
- **What**: Battle-tested financial model templates (Excel/Sheets) — revenue forecasts, expenses, hiring, runway, cap table. Used by 91,000+ founders
- **Action**: Buy Standard Financial Model ($295) and Cap Table/Exit Waterfall Tool. Build 5-year model with unit economics per dish scored
- **Cost**: $295 one-time

### Puzzle
- **What**: Startup accounting with real-time burn rate, runway, and ARR metrics
- **Action**: Switch bookkeeping immediately. Investors will ask for financial statements — Puzzle generates them automatically
- **Cost**: Free tier available

### Papermark
- **What**: Open-source DocSend alternative — share deck via trackable links, see which slides investors read, custom domains
- **Action**: Upload pitch deck. Create unique trackable link per investor. Monitor engagement daily during raise
- **Cost**: Free
- **Alternative**: DocSend ($150+/mo) if you want the brand recognition investors expect

### Evalyze.ai
- **What**: AI pitch deck analyzer + investor matching. Flags gaps investors will notice (unclear TAM, missing GTM, incomplete financials)
- **Action**: Upload current deck for AI review. Fix flagged issues before sending to investors. Use matching for food-tech investors
- **Cost**: Free tier

### Get Startup Funding + Pitch Deck Hunt
- **What**: Libraries of real pitch decks from funded startups (successes and failures)
- **Action**: Study 10+ consumer/food-tech decks. Note TAM slide structure, traction metrics, and ask slide
- **Cost**: Free

### Startup Fundraising Calculator
- **What**: Model ownership dilution, SAFE/KISS conversions, option pools, scenario planning
- **Action**: Run scenarios now — what does $500K on SAFE at $5M cap vs $8M cap do to ownership? What about $1.5M?
- **Cost**: Free

### Clerky or Stripe Atlas
- **What**: Delaware C-Corp incorporation, SAFE document generation, 83(b) filing
- **Action**: If not yet incorporated as Delaware C-Corp, do so immediately. Use for generating SAFE documents when investors sign
- **Cost**: Clerky ~$800, Stripe Atlas $500
- **Note**: Stripe Atlas includes $5K+ in partner credits (AWS, etc.)

### Mercury
- **What**: Startup banking with APIs, virtual cards, treasury management, built-in investor database
- **Action**: Open account. Park operational funds and raise proceeds here
- **Cost**: Free

## TIER 2 — Use During Active Fundraise

### OpenVC
- **What**: Free database of 20,000+ verified investors (VCs, angels, family offices, accelerators) with built-in CRM
- **Action**: Filter for pre-seed/seed food-tech, consumer app, and Philadelphia-area investors. Build target list of 100+
- **Cost**: Free

### Signal by NFX
- **What**: AI tool that maps relationships between founders, investors, and operators to find warm intro paths
- **Action**: Create profile. Map your network. Find second-degree connections to food-tech investors. Warm intros convert 10x vs cold
- **Cost**: Free

### AngelMatch
- **What**: Database of 110K+ investors with verified emails, investment focus, past investments, locations
- **Action**: Subscribe during raise. Filter for food/beverage, consumer, mobile app investors. Export contact lists
- **Cost**: $59–149/month

### Slidebean
- **What**: AI pitch deck creator with 120+ templates from funded startups, view tracking, investor finder
- **Action**: Use consumer app/marketplace template. Upload ForkFox metrics. Use view tracking to see which investors engaged
- **Cost**: $49+/month

### Flowlie
- **What**: All-in-one fundraising hub — round planning, investor discovery, one-pager builder, outreach management
- **Action**: Create ForkFox one-pager using their template. Use AI investor discovery for food-tech matches
- **Cost**: Free tier available

### Forecastr
- **What**: Custom financial models built with analyst support — they pair you with a professional analyst
- **Action**: Have analyst build model showing cost-per-dish-score, restaurant adoption curve in Philadelphia, path to profitability
- **Cost**: ~$500+/month

### Crunchbase (free tier)
- **What**: Database of companies, investors, funding rounds, industry trends
- **Action**: Research which VCs recently invested in food-tech/restaurant-tech. Note check sizes and valuations for competitive landscape
- **Cost**: Free tier

### Pulley
- **What**: Cap table management with pre-close dilution preview — shows how fundraising affects ownership before you close
- **Action**: Model SAFE terms before signing. Preview ownership impact at different scenarios
- **Cost**: Free tier available

### Digify / Papermark Data Room
- **What**: Virtual data room for due diligence — watermarking, NDA management, document tracking
- **Action**: Set up when investors reach DD stage (patent docs, incorporation docs, financials)
- **Cost**: Free–$50/month

### HubSpot for Startups
- **What**: Free CRM with fundraising templates and up to 90% startup discount
- **Action**: Apply for startup discount. Use fundraising pipeline templates if you prefer HubSpot over OpenVC
- **Cost**: Free (with startup discount)

## TIER 3 — Nice to Have / Post-Raise

### Wefunder / Republic / StartEngine
- **What**: Equity crowdfunding platforms — let anyone invest as little as $50–100
- **When**: After or alongside VC raise
- **Why**: Let your 2,490+ dish-scoring users invest. Good PR story for a consumer food app
- **Cost**: Platform takes 5–7% of raise

### Visible.vc
- **What**: Investor relationship hub — send monthly updates, share data room, track engagement
- **When**: After closing round
- **Why**: Clean monthly updates build trust for Series A. Investors judge you on communication

### Brex
- **What**: Corporate card with 10–20x limits, startup rewards, $5K+ partner credits (AWS, etc.)
- **When**: After closing raise
- **Why**: High limits for ad spend, AWS credits, startup-optimized rewards

### Beautiful.ai / Gamma / Storydoc
- **What**: Alternative deck builders (AI-powered, interactive, web-based)
- **When**: If deck needs a refresh or you want an interactive web version
- **Why**: Gamma's scrollable web deck stands out on mobile. Storydoc embeds video + booking links

### Foundersuite
- **What**: Fundraising CRM with 216K+ investor database
- **When**: Series A or larger raises ($5M+)
- **Why**: Overkill for seed but powerful for bigger raises

### Funden Assisted Fundraising
- **What**: Managed fundraising — they perfect your pitch and provide warm intros to 500+ partner VCs
- **When**: If DIY fundraising isn't working or you're time-constrained
- **Why**: They do the outreach for you

### AngelList
- **What**: Platform for incorporation, banking, equity, fundraising, hiring, and angel syndicates
- **When**: Series A and beyond
- **Why**: Find food-tech angel syndicates. Some investors actively browse for deals

### PitchBob
- **What**: AI chatbot generates 12-slide deck, one-pager, elevator pitch video script, plus database of 150K+ VCs
- **When**: Supplementary to your main deck
- **Why**: Quick AI-generated one-pager and VC database access

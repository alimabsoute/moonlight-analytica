# Tastyr IQ — FOMO Campaign Playbook

## Operating Principle

**FOMO is not a tactic — it's the operating system.** Every touchpoint, every message, every public action is designed to create the perception that Tastyr IQ is inevitable, that the smart money is already paying attention, and that hesitation = missing out.

---

## Section 1: Scarcity Tactics

### 1.1 Limited Beta Cohort
**Tactic**: "Only 50 spots for Philly restaurateurs in our founding cohort"
- Create numbered waitlist (publicly visible count)
- Each restaurateur gets a unique "Founding Partner" designation
- Announce cohort closings: "Only 12 spots remaining"
- After cohort fills, announce "waitlist now open for Cohort 2"

**Execution**:
- Landing page with live counter: "47/50 spots claimed"
- Each accepted restaurateur gets a certificate/badge for their window
- Social posts: "Welcome [Restaurant X] to the Tastyr IQ Founding Cohort"

### 1.2 Founding Investor Title
**Tactic**: "Founding Investor" status for early seed participants
- Limited to first $250K committed
- Includes advisory board seat, quarterly dinners with founders
- Public recognition on the app's "Our Backers" page
- First access to all future rounds

**Language**:
- "We're offering Founding Investor status to our first 5 investors"
- "Founding Investors get advisory board seats — only 5 available"
- "Once these 5 seats are filled, later investors won't get this designation"

### 1.3 Launch City Exclusivity
**Tactic**: "Launch City: Philadelphia" — Philly gets it first
- Philly is positioned as the CHOSEN city, not just where the founders happen to be
- "We analyzed 50 cities and chose Philadelphia for our launch because..."
- Other cities (NYC, DC, Boston) framed as "coming next" — creates FOMO in those markets too
- Philly investors get "hometown advantage" positioning

**Language**:
- "Philadelphia was chosen as our launch city because of its world-class food scene"
- "Before we expand to NYC, we're perfecting the model here"
- "Philly investors have a unique window to get in before national expansion"

### 1.4 Round Structure Scarcity
**Tactic**: "We're only meeting with 10 investors this round"
- Frame the round as oversubscribed (even early on)
- Create selection criteria: "We're choosing investors who bring strategic value, not just capital"
- Time pressure: "We're closing the round by [date]"

**Language**:
- "We're being selective — we want investors who understand the food industry"
- "Our round is structured for 5-8 investors max"
- "We've already had conversations with [X] investors and expect to close by [date]"

## Section 2: Social Proof Manufacturing

### 2.1 Coordinated Influencer Drop
**Tactic**: 5-10 food influencers all post about Tastyr IQ in the same 48-hour window

**Execution Timeline**:
1. **Day -14**: Identify and reach out to 10-15 influencers with beta access offer
2. **Day -7**: Onboard accepted influencers, give full beta access
3. **Day -3**: Share "suggested posting window" (not mandatory — keep it organic)
4. **Day 0-1**: Influencers post their Tastyr IQ experiences
5. **Day 2-3**: Ali reposts/engages with all content
6. **Day 4-5**: Pitch media: "Philadelphia food influencers are buzzing about this new AI app"

**Why It Works**:
- Creates perception of organic momentum
- Algorithms boost content when multiple creators post about same topic
- Media sees "trend" and wants to cover it
- Investors see "market pull" not "push"

### 2.2 "As Seen In" Press Momentum
**Tactic**: Build press mentions BEFORE approaching investors

**Priority Press Targets** (in order):
1. **Eater Philly** — "New AI app is rating Philly's best dishes" (food-native audience)
2. **Technical.ly Philly** — "Philly startup uses AI to rate individual dishes" (tech angle)
3. **PhillyMag** — "This app scores your favorite restaurant's dishes" (consumer angle)
4. **BillyPenn** — "Two founders chose Philly to launch their food-tech startup" (local pride)
5. **Philadelphia Inquirer** — Food desk feature

**Post-Coverage**:
- Add press logos to pitch deck
- "As featured in Eater Philly, Technical.ly, PhillyMag"
- Share coverage in investor outreach emails
- Post coverage to LinkedIn (investors follow LinkedIn)

### 2.3 Restaurateur Case Studies
**Tactic**: Document every restaurateur interaction as a case study

**Format**:
```
CASE STUDY: [Restaurant Name]
Chef [Name] learned their [Dish] scored 94/100 on Tastyr IQ
"I had no idea our khao soi was our highest-rated dish" — [Quote]
Result: Menu featured dish prominently, 23% increase in orders
```

**Usage**:
- Include in pitch deck (1-2 case studies)
- Share in investor emails
- Post to LinkedIn
- Provide to media as story angles

### 2.4 App Store Presence
**Tactic**: Leverage App Store approval as credibility stamp

**Language**:
- "Already approved and live on the App Store"
- "You can download it right now" (link in every email)
- "Join our TestFlight beta for early access to upcoming features"
- Screenshot the App Store listing — use in all materials

## Section 3: Exclusivity Signals

### 3.1 Invite-Only Beta Language
All outreach uses exclusivity framing:

| Instead of... | Say... |
|-------------|--------|
| "Try our app" | "You've been selected for our invite-only beta" |
| "Sign up" | "Claim your spot in our founding cohort" |
| "Download the app" | "Your exclusive beta access is ready" |
| "We'd love your feedback" | "We're only sharing this with 10 industry leaders" |
| "Can we meet?" | "We're meeting with a small group of selected investors" |

### 3.2 NDA/Patent Framing
**Tactic**: Use patent status to create confidentiality mystique

**Language**:
- "Our scoring algorithm is patent-pending — we can share details under NDA"
- "We're being selective about who we discuss our proprietary technology with"
- "As a Founding Investor, you'd get full access to our patent filing"

### 3.3 "Chosen" Language
Position everyone as specially selected:

- **Investors**: "We specifically identified you because of your [specific investment/background]"
- **Restaurateurs**: "Your restaurant was one of 5 selected for our founding cohort"
- **Influencers**: "You're one of the first people in Philadelphia to see this"
- **Media**: "We're offering you an exclusive first-look before public launch"
- **Academics**: "We're partnering with only 3 universities in our pilot program"

## Section 4: FOMO-Specific Messaging by Segment

### Investors
**Primary FOMO trigger**: Missing the next big food-tech platform

> "Our seed round is actively filling — we're prioritizing investors who understand the Philadelphia food scene and can add strategic value beyond capital. We've already had productive conversations with [X] other investors in the ecosystem, and we expect to close within [timeframe]. Given your background in [specific area], you'd be a natural fit for our Founding Investor cohort — but those seats are limited."

**Follow-up FOMO**:
> "Quick update — we've received commitments for [X]% of our round since we last spoke. Wanted to give you priority before we close the remaining allocation."

### Restaurateurs
**Primary FOMO trigger**: Competitors are already being rated

> "We've already indexed your competitors' top dishes — [Competitor Restaurant A]'s [dish] scored 91/100 and [Competitor B]'s [dish] scored 87/100. We haven't rated your dishes yet because we wanted to invite you to our Founding Cohort first. Want to see how your menu stacks up?"

**Follow-up FOMO**:
> "[Competitor Restaurant] just joined our founding cohort. They're already seeing which of their dishes rank highest. Only [X] spots remaining for Philadelphia restaurants."

### Food Bloggers / Influencers
**Primary FOMO trigger**: Being first, having exclusive content

> "You'll be one of the first 5 people in Philadelphia to use Tastyr IQ — before it's available to the public. Imagine being the first to show your followers that [Famous Restaurant]'s top-rated dish is actually their [surprising dish], not what everyone assumes. This is exclusive content no one else will have."

**Follow-up FOMO**:
> "Just FYI — [@CompetitorInfluencer] just posted their Tastyr IQ review and it's blowing up. We still have your invite-only access waiting..."

### Media / Journalists
**Primary FOMO trigger**: Missing a breaking story

> "We're offering an exclusive first-look at Tastyr IQ before our public launch. We've already briefed [other publication] but haven't given anyone the full story yet. Given your coverage of [specific beat], you'd be the perfect person to break this — an AI platform that rates individual dishes, not restaurants. Patent-pending technology, 2,490+ dishes already indexed, and Philadelphia's top chefs are already paying attention."

**Embargo tactic**:
> "We can offer you an exclusive under embargo — 48 hours before anyone else publishes."

### University / Academic
**Primary FOMO trigger**: Being part of cutting-edge food-tech research

> "We're partnering with only 3 universities for our founding research program. Given [University]'s strength in [food science/entrepreneurship/AI], you'd be an ideal partner. Our dataset of 2,490+ dish ratings across 9 verticals is a goldmine for [specific research application]. We'd love to explore a research partnership before we finalize our university cohort."

## Section 7: Anti-Patterns — What NOT to Do

1. **Don't lie about traction** — Stretch the truth, but never fabricate investors or commitments
2. **Don't pressure too hard** — FOMO should feel organic, not desperate
3. **Don't burn bridges** — Every "not now" is a "maybe later"
4. **Don't oversaturate** — 3 influencer posts > 30 low-quality posts
5. **Don't ignore follow-up** — The FOMO dies without nurturing
6. **Don't use the same message twice** — Every touch should be unique and personalized
7. **Don't reveal the playbook** — Never say "we're creating FOMO" — let it feel natural
8. **Don't rush the chains** — The chain strategy requires each link to form before moving to the next

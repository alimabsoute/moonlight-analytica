The Problem

Your best dishes are invisible.

Yelp rates your restaurant. Google rates your restaurant. Nobody rates your $28 lamb chop. Until now.

📉

Aggregate ratings bury your stars

A 4.1-star restaurant average tells guests nothing about which dish to order. Your signature mushroom risotto is indistinguishable from your weakest item.

🔍

Dish-level discovery doesn't exist

When someone searches "best ramen in Philly," they find restaurants — not dishes. The best bowl in the city might be at a restaurant with 3.8 stars overall.

You know your bestsellers by volume. But do you know which dish makes guests come back? Which dish drives the highest check size? ForkFox gives you that data.

How It Works

Three steps to dish-level visibility.

No complex integration. No training required. Your menu is live on ForkFox in hours.

01

List your menu

Add your dishes to the ForkFox portal. We handle the rest — photography prompts, category tagging, cuisine mapping across 10 verticals.

02

AI scores every dish

Our patent-pending algorithm scores each dish across four dimensions: flavor accuracy, texture consistency, value match, and authenticity. Continuously updated as guests rate.

03

Your best dishes surface first

High-scoring dishes rise to the top of ForkFox discovery. When someone searches "best birria tacos in San Francisco," your 94-scored tacos appear first.

04

Track dish-level insights

Your restaurant dashboard shows which dishes are trending, which are declining, guest sentiment by dish, and which items drive repeat visits. Real data, not stars.

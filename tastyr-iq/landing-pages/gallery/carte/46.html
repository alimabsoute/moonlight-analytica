Best Pad Thai in New York

10 dishes ranked · Top score 93 · Elmhurst + Hell's Kitchen focus
FX
ForkFox Editorial · April 2026
93 Top Score  ·  10 Ranked  ·  1,100+ Reviews
New York's pad thai scene splits cleanly between two distinct worlds: the immigrant community restaurants of Elmhurst, Queens — where the dish is made for Thai diners who know what proper pad thai tastes like — and the Hell's Kitchen / Midtown corridor, where higher rents and tourist volume push operators toward sweeter, less complex interpretations.
The Rankings
1
Pad Thai Goong (Shrimp)
SriPraPhai — Elmhurst, Queens
The Elmhurst institution that Thai expats use as a reference point. Tamarind paste made in-house, palm sugar not white sugar, proper dried shrimp. The wok heat leaves visible char on the noodles.
93
2
Pad Thai Tofu (Traditional)
Ayada Thai — Elmhurst, Queens
Firm tofu, bean sprouts that stay crunchy, chives that are still bright. The fish sauce balance here is precise — funky but not overbearing. A vegetarian pad thai that doesn't compromise.
90
3
Pad Thai Duck
Pure Thai Cookhouse — Hell's Kitchen
The only Manhattan pad thai that competes with the Queens benchmark. Duck confit integrated into a proper tamarind-based sauce. Thin flat rice noodles cooked to the right al dente.
88
4
Pad Thai with Pork Belly
Fish Cheeks — NoHo
Contemporary Thai-American interpretation that earns its score through technique. The pork belly adds fat depth that the classic shrimp version lacks. Garnishes are intentional not decorative.
86
5
Pad Thai Combo
Pam Real Thai — Hell's Kitchen
Family-run since 2001. The combo version (shrimp + chicken + tofu) allows tasting across proteins. Smaller portions than competitors but higher flavor concentration per bite.
84
Elmhurst has more quality Thai restaurants per block than any neighborhood outside Bangkok or LA's Thai Town. The QD corridor from Roosevelt to Woodside is the most underrated food street in New York.
ForkFox Editorial · April 2026
6
Pad Thai Seafood
Pokpok NY — Columbia Street Waterfront
Andy Ricker's Brooklyn outpost earns consistent scores for technique. The seafood version uses squid, shrimp, and scallop. Rice noodles are slightly wider than standard for better sauce absorption.
82
7
Vegetarian Pad Thai Deluxe
Wondee Siam — Hell's Kitchen
Best vegetarian-only pad thai in the city. The owner refuses to use oyster sauce, using a mushroom-based umami substitute. Result scores near traditional versions on flavor.
80
8
Pad Thai Classic
Lotus of Siam — Midtown East
NYC satellite of the Las Vegas institution. Consistent, technically correct, but the Midtown location adds a 25% premium without a comparable quality lift over Queens alternatives.
78
9
Pad Thai Roll (Fusion)
Obao — Upper East Side
Hybrid presentation — pad thai noodle components wrapped in a rice paper roll with mango. Creative and consistent, penalized on authenticity but scores well on texture and value.
75
10
Pad Thai with Crab
Somtum Der — East Village
Isaan-style restaurant that does pad thai as a supporting item, not a signature. The crab version is premium but inconsistent across visits — drops points on reliability.
73

Best Ramen in Los Angeles

10 dishes rankedTop score 95Sawtelle + Little TokyoApril 2026
FX
ForkFox Editorial · April 2026
95 top score · 10 ranked · 2,400+ reviews
Los Angeles has the most competitive ramen market in the United States. The density of Japanese chain outposts, independent operators, and Japanese-American fusion spots along the Sawtelle Corridor and Little Tokyo creates a competitive pressure that has produced a measurably higher floor of quality compared to any other American city — including New York.
The Rankings
1
Spicy Torigara Tonkotsu
Noodle Boy — Sawtelle Corridor
The Sawtelle benchmark. Chef Kenta Takahashi blends tori (chicken) and tonkotsu bases in a 60/40 ratio that produces a broth simultaneously light and rich. The house-made la-yu chili oil is compulsory.
95
2
Black Garlic Tonkotsu (Kuro)
Shin-Sen-Gumi — Gardena
Hakata-style institution with 20+ years of consistency. The Gardena original outperforms the newer locations by a measurable margin. The kuro (black garlic oil) version is the prestige order.
93
3
Shoyu Chashu Ramen
Yamadaya — Culver City
Hakata-style tonkotsu with a shoyu tare (seasoning) adjustment that produces a cleaner, more umami-forward broth. The chashu here is 16-hour sous vide pork belly, finished on the grill.
91
LA's ramen market is uniquely defined by its Japanese diaspora population. The Sawtelle Corridor is effectively a Japanese-American food street — the density and authenticity of operators here is something no other American city can replicate.
ForkFox Editorial · April 2026
4
Chicken Paitan (White Broth)
Tsujita Annex — Sawtelle
The tsukemen (dipping) specialist also runs a paitan broth ramen. The chicken-based white broth is cooked to a milky opacity through emulsification — technically distinct from tonkotsu but equally rich.
89
5
Mazemen (Soupless)
Jinya Ramen Bar — West Hollywood
The mazemen — soupless noodles in concentrated sauce — is the most technically demanding dish on this list. Jinya's version uses a truffle-infused sauce base that earns strong presentation and flavor marks.
87
6
Vegan Shio Ramen
Tatsu Ramen — Los Feliz
The best vegan ramen in the United States. Kombu and shiitake dashi base with a shio tare that achieves salt precision usually only possible with fish-based broths. The plant-based chashu actually works.
85
7
Wagyu Beef Tallow Ramen
Chaumont — Little Tokyo
Modern omakase-style ramen counter. The wagyu tallow floats on a clean chicken broth — more visual than functional, but the overall bowl quality is consistently high. Value score drops due to $28 price point.
83
8
Hakata Classic Tonkotsu
Ippudo — Little Tokyo
The Japanese chain that introduced omakase-style ramen to the US market. The Little Tokyo location holds quality standards closer to the Japanese original than the NYC branch. Consistent over 8+ years.
81
9
Curry Ramen
Ramen Nagi — Sawtelle
The Japanese curry ramen variation — a Sapporo-area tradition applied here with a Kare (curry) tare over tonkotsu. Spice level customizable. Appeals to the Sawtelle corridor's fusion-open crowd.
79
10
Cold Tsukemen (Dipping)
Tsujita LA — Sawtelle
The LA tsukemen pioneer. Thick noodles, concentrated broth served separately for dipping. The citrus tare finish is a house technique. Penalized slightly on value ($24+) but technique is impeccable.
77

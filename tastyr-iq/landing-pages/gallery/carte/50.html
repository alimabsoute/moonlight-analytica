Los Angeles is home to Little Ethiopia — a six-block stretch on Fairfax Avenue between Olympic and Pico that houses the highest concentration of Ethiopian restaurants in the Western United States. The neighborhood emerged in the late 1980s and has produced a generation of Ethiopian-American chefs who cook with imported spices, traditionally fermented injera, and techniques that trace directly to regional Ethiopian cooking traditions.
The Rankings
1
Doro Wat — Chicken in Spiced Berbere
Meals by Genet — Little Ethiopia, Fairfax
Chef Genet Agonafer is the James Beard-nominated chef who put Little Ethiopia on the national food map. Her doro wat uses three types of dried chiles in the berbere, niter kibbeh made fresh weekly, and eggs that simmer in the sauce for six hours. This is the reference point for Ethiopian cooking in the United States.
96
2
Kitfo — Ethiopian Beef Tartare
Rahel Ethiopian Vegan — Fairfax
Counter-intuitively, the best kitfo in LA comes from a vegan restaurant that makes a tofu-based version seasoned identically to the beef original. The mitmita (spice blend) and niter kibbeh (vegan butter) are house-made and indistinguishable from the traditional preparation in blind taste tests.
93
3
Tibs Zilzil — Beef Strip Sauté
Nyala Ethiopian — Fairfax
Thin-sliced beef strips sautéed with awaze (chili paste), rosemary, and fresh ginger over an open flame. Nyala has maintained this preparation unchanged for over 20 years. The charred edges and the residual heat in the iron skillet that continues cooking at the table are the distinguishing quality markers.
91
Little Ethiopia on Fairfax is the single most important block in American Ethiopian cuisine. Every significant Ethiopian-American chef in the United States has either trained here, eaten here, or been shaped by what Genet Agonafer established at Meals by Genet starting in 1996.

4
Beyaynetu Combination Platter
Merkato — Little Ethiopia
The standard Ethiopian introduction — eight dishes on one injera canvas. Merkato's version includes three rotating seasonal dishes that change monthly. The injera is fermented for 72 hours minimum, producing the sour tang that's essential to authentic preparation.
89
5
Lamb Shank Alicha
Hawwi Ethiopian — Mid-Wilshire
Alicha is the mild counterpart to the spice-forward wats — braised with turmeric, ginger, and onion rather than berbere. The lamb shank preparation here is a 5-hour braise that produces fall-off-bone texture with a broth that's served alongside for dipping.
87
6
Shiro Wat — Chickpea Flour Stew
Rosalind's Ethiopian — Fairfax
The oldest restaurant in Little Ethiopia. Rosalind's shiro recipe has been unchanged since 1992. Ground chickpea and fava bean flour cooked with berbere into a dense paste. The version here is the community standard that all LA competitors are measured against.
85
7
Gored Gored — Cubed Raw Beef
Meals by Genet — Little Ethiopia
Raw cubed beef tossed with niter kibbeh and mitmita. Less processed than kitfo, preserving more beef texture. Available only on weekends when the beef sourcing from a Certified Ethiopian farm in Fresno County is available.
83
8
Misir Alicha — Red Lentil Stew
Ethiopian Restaurant — Culver City
The most accessible Ethiopian dish and the highest-value proposition on this list at $11. The Culver City location serves the largest portions of any restaurant on this ranking. The lentils are properly spiced — not dumbed down for a non-Ethiopian audience.
81
9
Asa Tibs — Pan-Fried Fish
Awash — Koreatown
Ethiopian fish preparation using Pacific tilapia with a niter kibbeh and jalapeño sauce. The Koreatown crossover demographic has made Awash an interesting cultural fusion point — but the cooking stays strictly Ethiopian in technique.
79
10
Honey Wine (Tej) Braised Lamb
Messob Ethiopian — Culver City
A modern restaurant offering that braised lamb shoulder in tej (Ethiopian honey wine) — a preparation common at feast occasions in Ethiopia but rarely seen in restaurant settings in the US. Creative and technically proficient.
77
Key Findings
Little Ethiopia on Fairfax has 6 of the top 10 spots — the most concentrated quality corridor of any American food street in any cuisine
Meals by Genet is the national benchmark — no other US Ethiopian restaurant has produced comparable critical recognition over 30 years
Vegan Ethiopian performs at par with meat versions — LA's Rahel proves plant-based preparation can match traditional kitfo
Injera fermentation time is the single best predictor of overall quality — 72-hour minimum separates the top tier from the rest

Best Tacos in New York

10 dishes rankedTop score 94Jackson Heights + Sunset ParkApril 2026
FX
ForkFox Editorial · April 2026
94 top score  ·  10 ranked  ·  1,500+ reviews
New York's taco scene is anchored in neighborhoods where Mexican immigrants have maintained regional cooking traditions without accommodation to the mainstream market: Jackson Heights in Queens, Sunset Park in Brooklyn, and the Bronx's Kingsbridge corridor. The quality gap between these community spots and the tourist-facing spots in Manhattan is wide and consistent.
The Rankings
1
Al Pastor Taco
Los Tacos No. 1 — Chelsea Market
The only Manhattan taco spot that earns a top score without apology. Trompo al pastor spinning in the window, Oaxacan cheese melted to order, achiote that comes from actual dried chiles, not paste. The line is long and worth it.
94
2
Birria de Res Taco
La Chula NYC — Jackson Heights
Consomé so complex it's worth ordering as a standalone. The quesabirria variant — cheese-crusted tortilla dipped in the broth — is the proper NYC expression of a technique LA pioneered.
91
3
Carne Asada Taco
Tortilleria Nixtamal — Corona, Queens
The masa is ground in-house — a distinction that separates Nixtamal from every other NYC taco spot. The carne asada gets the char that requires an open-flame grill, not a flat-top.
89
The best tacos in New York are in neighborhoods where Mexican families live, not neighborhoods where tourists eat. Jackson Heights is a 25-minute subway ride from Midtown and worth every minute.
ForkFox Editorial · April 2026
4
Carnitas Taco
El Cochinito — Washington Heights
Pork slow-cooked in its own fat in a copper pot visible from the street. Washington Heights has a Puerto Rican and Dominican majority but El Cochinito's Mexican owner has produced the best carnitas in NYC for a decade.
87
5
Lengua Taco
Taqueria El Rancho — Sunset Park
Beef tongue braised low and slow, diced and griddle-crisped to order. Sunset Park's Mexican community has maintained authentic cooking standards that Williamsburg restaurants across the bridge charge double for.
85
6
Cabeza Taco
Taqueria Coatzingo — Woodside
Beef head — cheeks, tongue, and slow-braised snout. The most underordered taco in NYC and one of the most flavorful. Coatzingo is the Woodside benchmark for traditional taqueria style.
83
7
Chorizo Taco
Casa Adela — Alphabet City
Puerto Rican-run spot with a credible Mexican menu. The chorizo here is loose, house-seasoned, and fried with potato. Lower authenticity score compensated by exceptional value and flavor.
81
8
Fish Taco Baja Style
Tacombi — Nolita
The most tourist-friendly spot on this list, but the Baja fish taco with battered mahi and chipotle crema earns a consistent score. The tortillas are fresh masa, not from a bag.
79
9
Suadero Taco
Azteca — Kingsbridge, Bronx
Suadero — a cut between the belly and the leg — slow cooked in lard. Fatty, rich, and deeply savory. The Bronx Mexican community produces honest food at price points that Manhattan can't match.
77
10
Veggie Mushroom Taco
Oxomoco — Greenpoint
Wood-fire Mexican restaurant where the mushroom taco is a serious dish. Maitake and trumpet mushrooms roasted in the wood-burning oven with epazote and jalapeño. Best vegetarian taco in the city.
75

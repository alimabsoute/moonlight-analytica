Best Sushi in Chicago

10 dishes rankedTop score 92River North + West LoopApril 2026
FX
ForkFox Editorial · April 2026
92 top score · 10 ranked · 1,300+ reviews
Chicago's sushi scene has historically been underrated relative to NYC and LA, but the River North and West Loop corridors have produced a cluster of high-performing operators since 2020 that have changed the competitive landscape. The city's Japanese-American chef community is small but tightly connected, with training lineages that trace back to both Nobu and Masa in New York.
The Rankings
1
Omakase Nigiri Set (15 Pieces)
Kyoten — West Loop
The most acclaimed sushi in the Midwest. Chef Otto Phan's edomae technique applied to Midwestern ingredients — including lake-caught perch nigiri that's become a Chicago signature. 30-seat counter, months-long wait list.
92
2
Otoro Nigiri (Bluefin Fatty Tuna)
Momotaro — West Loop
Izakaya-style restaurant with a serious sushi bar program. Boka Restaurant Group's sourcing network reaches Tsukiji-connected distributors. The otoro arrives at 62°F — the optimal temperature that most restaurants miss.
89
3
Hamachi Yellowtail Sashimi
Sushi-san — River North
Casual high-volume sushi that punches above its weight. The hamachi here is sourced from Japanese farms and arrives twice weekly. The ponzu-jalapeño treatment is a Chicago adaptation that scores well despite diverging from tradition.
87
River North has more high-performing sushi per block than any other non-coastal American city. The West Loop is catching up fast. Chicago has quietly become the Midwest's answer to the NYC sushi corridor — at 30% lower prices.
ForkFox Editorial · April 2026
4
Spicy Tuna Roll
Macku — Lincoln Park
Chef Macku Chan has been making the same spicy tuna recipe for 22 years. House-made kewpie and sriracha blend, sushi-grade bluefin, crispy shallots. Consistency is the benchmark metric here.
85
5
Scallop Butter Aburi
Naoki Sushi — Bucktown
Torch-seared scallop with yuzu butter and micro-shiso. The aburi (flame-kissed) technique is properly executed — light char, not a cook-through. One of few Chicago spots doing this correctly.
83
6
Salmon Belly Nigiri
Arami — Bucktown
Japanese-Italian fusion restaurant with a focused nigiri list. The salmon belly is Norwegian farm-raised and treated with a miso cure before serving. Non-traditional but consistent and high-scoring.
81
7
Uni Temaki
Juno — Lincoln Park
Sea urchin hand roll with seaweed crisped to order. The Santa Barbara uni sourcing here is a premium decision that shows in flavor. Hand rolls lose texture after 60 seconds — Juno serves them immediately.
80
8
Dragon Roll
Coast Sushi Bar — Bucktown
Tempura shrimp + avocado + eel sauce. The classic American sushi roll done well at a price point ($16) that beats River North by a significant margin. Fresh avocado sourced daily makes the difference.
78
9
Rainbow Roll
En Hakkore — Pilsen
Japanese-Korean fusion spot in Pilsen with a sincere sushi menu. The rainbow roll here uses local ingredients on top — including Great Lakes whitefish. An interesting Chicago-specific take.
76
10
Chirashi Bowl
Bob San — Wicker Park
Affordable chirashi with 9 proteins over sushi rice. Not the most prestigious sourcing on this list but the most accessible — walk-ins available seven days a week at $28, comparable to $48 chirashi elsewhere.
74

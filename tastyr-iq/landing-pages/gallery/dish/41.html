Philadelphia's Chinatown Has a Dim Sum Kitchen That Hasn't Been Written About Enough

ForkFox Algorithm March 2026 · 5 min read · Score: 86

Philadelphia's Chinatown — concentrated on and around Race Street between 9th and 11th — is smaller than Chicago's or San Francisco's, which means the quality of each individual operation carries more weight per block. The dim sum kitchen our model scored highest here has been operating in the same space since 2004, and its customer base is almost entirely drawn from the Cantonese and Fujianese communities of the corridor.

The Har Gow Test

We use har gow — steamed shrimp dumplings — as the primary quality indicator for any dim sum kitchen. The wrapper must be translucent when steamed, flexible without tearing, and thick enough to hold its pleating. The shrimp filling must be whole pieces, not paste, with a clean snap when bitten. The Philadelphia kitchen scores 93 on har gow construction — our highest score for this item in the Northeast outside of New York's Flushing operations.

"A har gow wrapper that's too thin tears under the shrimp's weight. Too thick and it dominates the flavor and turns gummy. The correct spec is 1.2mm, rolled fresh, every morning."

The Turnip Cake Revelation

The most underscored item at this kitchen — and the one that separates it from comparable operations in the corridor — is the lo bak go, or turnip cake. Most dim sum turnip cakes are pan-fried slabs of daikon and rice flour paste that are dense, starchy, and texturally monotonous. The Philadelphia kitchen's version is made with a higher daikon-to-starch ratio, pan-fried in a thin oil layer that produces a crispy exterior without adding greasiness, and seasoned with lap cheong (Chinese sausage) that has been diced fine enough to distribute evenly through every bite. It is the best turnip cake in the city and possibly the best in the Northeast. Score: 97 on this specific item.

The full dim sum service runs Saturday and Sunday from 9am to 2pm. The har gow, siu mai, turnip cake, and cheung fun are the essential orders. The egg tarts arrive at 10am. Be there before 11 or the turnip cake will be sold out.

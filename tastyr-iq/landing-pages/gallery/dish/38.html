Chicago's Argyle Street in Uptown is the Midwest's primary Vietnamese food corridor — a 4-block stretch of Vietnamese restaurants, bakeries, and grocery stores that has sustained a Vietnamese-American community since the late 1970s. Pho quality here is maintained by competition from a community that consumes the dish multiple times a week, which creates a quality floor that most American pho markets never achieve.

The 88 That Matters

Our highest-scoring pho on Argyle Street scored 88 on the composite — 96 on broth depth, 92 on broth clarity, 87 on noodle calibration, 85 on protein assembly, 88 on herb plate quality, and 84 on value index. The composite is lower than our LA leader (94) primarily because the value index is pulled down by price increases in 2025 that haven't been offset by quality improvements. The broth itself would score 94 in isolation. It is exceptional.

The broth has been on this stove, in this building, on this street, since 1983. The owners' children are now the cooks. The recipe hasn't moved.

The pho here is southern-style: wider noodles, a more complex broth with both bone and meat extraction stages, and a full herb plate with bean sprouts, basil, lime, and sliced jalapeño. The broth is a 14-hour process with a 45-minute skimming interval. The beef selection for the combination bowl — rare eye round, well-done flank, tendon, and tripe — is sourced from the Vietnamese butcher two doors west, which means the quality of the protein is vetted before it enters the building.

Argyle Street is 40 minutes from downtown Chicago by train. If you are in Chicago and have not been to this corridor for pho, you have not had Chicago pho. The model has spoken.

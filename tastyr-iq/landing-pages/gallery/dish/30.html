The Clarity Variable

A pho broth that scores 98 on clarity is a broth you can read through — hold it up to light and see through it the way you would through glass. Getting there requires 16 hours of disciplined low-heat cooking, consistent skimming every 45 minutes, and a final strain through fine-mesh muslin. The Westminster operation does all three. The result is the clearest pho broth in our Southern California dataset by a significant margin.

Left: the finished broth at service — clarity score 98. Center: the spice bouquet. Right: the beef before slicing.

The Spice Architecture

The aromatic backbone of a pho broth is built on a bouquet of charred onion and ginger, plus star anise, cinnamon sticks, cloves, coriander seeds, and cardamom. The charring of the onion and ginger is critical — it should be done over open flame until blackened on all exterior surfaces, which contributes a smoky sweetness that raw versions of these aromatics cannot provide. The Westminster kitchen chars both vegetables over a gas burner for 8 minutes before adding them to the stock pot.

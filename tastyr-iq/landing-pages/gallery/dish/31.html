The Olney neighborhood in North Philadelphia has a significant Korean population that has grown quietly over the past decade, building a restaurant ecosystem that serves a community with genuine culinary expectations. The Korean BBQ operation our model ranked first in the region is owned by a family from Busan who moved to Philadelphia in 2018 and opened in 2021.

The Galbi Standard

The galbi here is USDA Prime short rib, butterflied and cut to the Korean specification — 1/3 inch thick along the bone, bone-in. The marinade is a traditional base of Asian pear, soy sauce, sesame oil, garlic, and ginger, with the pear grated rather than juiced (the fiber slows the tenderizing effect, maintaining more structural integrity in the meat). Marinated for 24 hours minimum, which is the lower bound for proper flavor penetration at this cut thickness.

The charcoal is real charcoal — binchotan, not briquettes. The smoke character it produces is different in every measurable way from gas or charcoal briquettes. Our model records this as part of the grill technique score, and this kitchen's 92 on that dimension reflects both the fuel quality and the cook's management of the coals over the service period. The table-side grill rotation is managed by the kitchen, not by the guests, which is the correct approach for achieving consistent doneness.

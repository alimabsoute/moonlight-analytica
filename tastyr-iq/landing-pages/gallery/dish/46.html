What the Data Shows

The 20-point gap between the first and second-ranked operations in our Philadelphia ramen dataset is larger than the equivalent gap in any other city we've measured. This is partly a data artifact — the South Philly operation scores particularly high on broth construction (97), which is the most heavily weighted dimension — and partly genuine quality differentiation. The operation is operating at a level that the rest of the city hasn't caught up to.

The city average across all 26 operations is 67.3, with a standard deviation of 11.2. The top-ranked operation at 91 sits 2.1 standard deviations above the mean. In our dataset across all five cities, only three operations score this far above their local mean. It is a statistically unusual finding, and it reflects a kitchen that has been optimizing a single product — its tonkotsu broth — for seven years without distraction.

The Equipment Problem in New York

New York restaurant ranges are governed by BTU limits that make genuine wok hei difficult to achieve. Most Thai restaurants in the city are operating at 35,000 to 50,000 BTU per burner — enough for acceptable stir-fry, not enough for the caramelized, slightly smoky character that constitutes real wok hei.

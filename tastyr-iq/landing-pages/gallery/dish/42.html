The Neapolitan Pizza Score San Francisco Has Been Missing

After 56 pizza visits across the Bay Area, our model found one Mission District operation that achieves the four non-negotiable properties of proper Neapolitan simultaneously — and scores 91 as a result.

Score 91 ForkFox Algorithm · March 2026 · 6 min

San Francisco has had a complicated relationship with Neapolitan pizza. The city's sourdough culture and premium ingredients obsession produce technically impressive crusts, but the Neapolitan tradition — which requires specific flour (Tipo 00), specific fermentation (48 to 72 hours), specific temperature (850°F minimum), and specific cook time (60 to 90 seconds) — has remained elusive here. Most operations approximate one or two of these variables. The Mission kitchen our model ranked first achieves all four.

The 850°F Requirement

A true Neapolitan pizza is cooked at 850°F to 900°F in a wood-burning oven, which produces a cook time of 60 to 90 seconds. This brief, high-heat exposure creates a crust that is simultaneously charred in spots (the characteristic leopard spotting), soft and slightly chewy in the center, and light enough to fold and eat. A lower temperature oven cannot produce this result — longer baking at lower heat dries the crust before achieving the thermal shock that creates the texture. This kitchen uses a Stefano Ferrara oven imported from Naples. Wood only.

"60 to 90 seconds at 900°F. That window is the entire game. Miss it in either direction and you have a different pizza — possibly a good one, but not Neapolitan."

The Flour Specification

The dough uses Caputo Tipo 00 "Pizzeria" flour, a low-protein flour specifically milled for Neapolitan applications. Combined with a 72-hour cold fermentation, the dough develops a flavor complexity and a gluten structure that produces the characteristic extensibility — the dough stretches without tearing under the pizza maker's hands — and the open crumb structure in the cornicione (crust edge) that is the technical signature of the form. Score: 91. The Margherita with house-made fior di latte is the correct order.

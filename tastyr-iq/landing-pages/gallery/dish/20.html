A Sunset District Pad Thai That Benchmarks Wok Hei Above Every Restaurant in the City

One Thai operation in the Sunset District has installed commercial ring burners that output over 200,000 BTU. The dish it produces is unlike anything else in the Bay Area.

The Infrastructure Investment

Most restaurant ranges in the US output 35,000 to 60,000 BTU per burner. Commercial Thai ring burners — the type used in Thailand for street cooking and in authentic operations worldwide — output 100,000 to 200,000 BTU. The difference is not incremental. The extreme heat creates the caramelized, slightly smoky wok hei character that is the defining quality of properly executed Thai stir-fry. Without it, you have a warm noodle dish, not Pad Thai.

The Pad Thai Itself

The Sunset District operation's Pad Thai uses fresh rice noodles (not dried), fresh dayboat shrimp, and a tamarind sauce that is significantly more acidic than the Bay Area standard — closer to the Bangkok street style, which is noticeably tart before the sweetness arrives. The egg is scrambled into the noodles at the highest heat point, producing curds that are slightly charred on the outside and just-set inside. Dried shrimp and crushed peanuts added at plating.

What Tavern-Style Actually Means

Chicago tavern-style pizza — the kind that has been served in corner bars and neighborhood restaurants since the 1940s — is a thin, cracker-crisp crust cut into squares (not wedges) with a modest sauce and cheese application that lets the crust be the protagonist. It is the opposite of deep dish in every way, and it is what most Chicagoans actually eat most of the time. Our model has scored 44 tavern-style operations in the metro area.

"Tavern-style pizza is what Chicago actually eats. The deep dish performance is for everyone else — and the best thin crust in the city costs half as much."

The square-cut format of tavern-style pizza is not aesthetic preference — it's structural. The thin crust requires the smaller portion size to maintain integrity from cut to bite.

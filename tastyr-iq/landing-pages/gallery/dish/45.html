The Ramen Counter in Little Tokyo LA That Scores 95 and Has No Wait

95 ForkFox Algorithm · Ramen · Los Angeles · March 2026 · 6 min

Little Tokyo in downtown Los Angeles has the highest concentration of quality ramen operations west of New York, and the competition between those operations is intense enough that the quality floor is higher here than in any other American market. Our model scored 34 Little Tokyo ramen operations over 14 months. The composite leader — a 12-seat counter that has been open for eight years — scores 95 and, inexplicably, never has a wait longer than 20 minutes.

The Double-Broth Architecture

The bowl served here is a double-broth tonkotsu-shoyu hybrid: a base of tonkotsu (12-hour pork bone extraction) blended at service with a lighter chicken-shoyu broth, creating a composite that is rich but not opaque, umami-loaded but not one-dimensional. The proportions are 60% tonkotsu base, 40% shoyu, adjusted slightly per season. In winter, the ratio shifts toward more tonkotsu for additional richness. In summer, the shoyu component increases for a cleaner finish.

"60% tonkotsu / 40% shoyu, adjusted seasonally. The blending happens at service, not in advance — which means the kitchen has to do it correctly 80+ times per night."

The Aroma Oil

The finishing touch on each bowl is a drizzle of house-made aroma oil — a blend of rendered chicken fat, garlic, and a small amount of white sesame oil that arrives with the bowl still hot enough to produce a ring of oil that disperses on contact with the broth surface. The garlic in the aroma oil has been roasted rather than raw-infused, which provides depth without sharpness. It adds the final aromatic dimension that takes the bowl from 90 to 95.

Score: 95. The counter opens at 11:30am and serves until sold out, typically around 9pm. The tonkotsu-shoyu hybrid with extra chashu is the correct order. The supplementary noodle add-on — available for $3 — is always worth it. This is the ramen standard for the West Coast.

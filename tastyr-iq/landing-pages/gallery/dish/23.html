Chinatown Chicago's Best Egg Tart Is the Most Overlooked Dish in the Midwest

The Portuguese-style egg tart — glossy caramel custard in a crispy puff pastry shell — is the signature test item for any dim sum kitchen. In Chicago's Chinatown, one bakery has been making the country's best version since 2007.

Context

Chicago's Chinatown — centered on Wentworth Avenue and Cermak Road — is one of the oldest Chinese-American communities in the Midwest, with a concentration of Cantonese, Fujianese, and Shanghainese immigrants whose combined food culture represents multiple regional traditions. The dim sum operations here serve a mixed community of Cantonese residents and visitors, and the quality baseline is, as a result, higher than most comparable-sized Chinatown communities in secondary markets.

Our model scored 16 dim sum and bakery operations in the corridor over 14 months. The finding on egg tarts was the dataset's most surprising result — a gap of 22 points between the first and second-ranked addresses on this specific item.

The Egg Tart Standard

There are two styles of dim sum egg tart: the standard shortcrust pastry style (older, thicker, more crumbly) and the Portuguese-influenced puff pastry style with its signature caramelized custard top. The latter is the harder technical achievement and the one our model weights more heavily — the custard must have a slightly charred, slightly molten top surface while maintaining a clean set in the center.

The Custard Specification

The custard should be made from whole eggs (not separated, not augmented with cornstarch), evaporated milk, and sugar, baked at high temperature to achieve the characteristic charring on top. The finished tart should be served warm — custard that has cooled to room temperature loses its texture and the caramelized top begins to deflate. The bakery we scored highest serves tarts from the oven in 12-minute intervals throughout service.

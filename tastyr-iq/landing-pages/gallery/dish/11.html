The City's Best
Shoyu Ramen
Took 11 Years

A small Richmond District operation has spent over a decade calibrating a shoyu broth that no other kitchen in the Bay Area has matched. Our model finally has enough data to say so definitively.

Score: 92 ForkFox Algorithm · March 2026 · 5 min

Shoyu ramen is the most transparent bowl in the canon. With a clear chicken-soy broth and no heavy emulsification to hide behind, every technical decision is immediately legible in the finished product. A cloudy shoyu is a mistake. An under-seasoned one is laziness. A perfectly calibrated one tastes like it was made specifically for you.

Eleven Years of Calibration

The owner-chef opened in the Richmond District in 2015 with a broth she described in a 2017 interview as "not quite right." In 2019, she changed her soy tare formula. In 2022, she switched to a heritage-breed chicken supplier in Sonoma. In 2024, she adjusted the charred scallion proportion in the aromatic build. The bowl our model tasted in early 2026 reflects every one of those iterations, and it shows.

What the Score Reflects

Our composite score of 92 reflects: broth clarity (98), broth depth (94), noodle calibration (89), tare integration (95), topping quality (88), and value index (90). The shoyu tare is the signature variable — a blend of three soy varieties (white, aged, and tamari) that creates a saltiness that rounds rather than sharpens, with a fermented depth that takes several sips to fully register.

The chashu is rolled pork belly braised for 4 hours, not 2. The menma — bamboo shoots — are fermented in-house rather than purchased pre-processed. The soft-boiled egg sits in a shoyu-mirin marinade for exactly 6 hours, producing a jammy yolk with a faint brown ring that means the timing is right. Every component has been optimized. The bowl is exceptional.

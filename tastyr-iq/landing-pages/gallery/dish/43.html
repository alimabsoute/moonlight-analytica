Chicago's Albany Park Has Korean BBQ That Outscores Half of NYC Koreatown

Score 88 ForkFox Algorithm · March 2026 · 5 min

Albany Park is a Northwest Side Chicago neighborhood with a significant Korean-American population — part of the broader Korean corridor on Lawrence Avenue that stretches into Ravenswood. The Korean restaurants here serve a community that eats Korean food as a regular practice, not as an occasion, which creates the quality pressure that produces high scores. Our model found a Korean BBQ operation in this corridor scoring 88 on the composite — high enough to compete with the established Koreatown NY operations in our dataset.

The Charcoal Setup

The operation uses a mixed charcoal approach: binchotan white charcoal for the initial high-heat sear of the galbi, and compressed hardwood charcoal for sustaining the table temperature through the meal. The binchotan sears at approximately 1,000°F — hot enough to caramelize the pear-soy marinade on the exterior of the meat in under 2 minutes. The cook switches to maintenance charcoal after the initial sear to prevent over-charring while continuing to impart smoke character.

"The binchotan sears at 1,000°F. That initial caramelization of the pear-soy marinade in the first 90 seconds is the flavor event the rest of the meal builds on."

The Banchan Program

The banchan set here includes 10 items on weekends: kimchi (2-week minimum fermentation), oi sobagi (stuffed cucumber), kongnamul, japchae (glass noodles), pajeon (green onion pancake, made to order), seasoned spinach, dried seaweed salad, and three rotating seasonal items. The pajeon arriving from the kitchen to order — rather than pre-made and held — is the single most reliable quality signal in Korean dining. This kitchen does it correctly.

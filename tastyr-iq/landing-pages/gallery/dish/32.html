The Outer Sunset Thai kitchen that produced our highest Pad Thai score in the Bay Area dataset has installed commercial ring burners producing over 180,000 BTU per burner. This is not an incremental equipment upgrade — it is a categorical one. The heat differential between this kitchen and a standard restaurant range is the difference between a caramelized, smoky, wok-hei-forward Pad Thai and a warm noodle dish.

What 96 on Wok Hei Means

Our wok hei dimension measures the presence and quality of the Maillard-reaction-derived flavor compounds that result from extremely high-heat stir-frying. We evaluate this through aroma, surface char distribution on the noodles, and the clean, slightly smoky aftertaste that distinguishes proper wok-cooked food from lower-heat approximations. A score of 96 means the characteristic is clearly, definitively present across every order, which requires the equipment to be capable and the cook to be technically skilled in its use.

The Wagyu Question

Several Koreatown LA operations now offer wagyu-grade galbi at premium prices — anywhere from $85 to $160 per pound. The scoring question is whether the wagyu specification is being used to justify price inflation or whether it represents a genuine quality step. Our model found significant variance: three operations charge wagyu prices for product our scoring data would call upper-Prime at best. Two operations deliver wagyu-level intramuscular fat distribution, tenderness, and flavor.

"Wagyu grade is a sourcing claim — our model measures it at the table through fat distribution scoring, tenderness calibration, and flavor depth. Two of nine operations passed."

The table setup at the top-ranked operation: dedicated charcoal brazier per table, chef-managed grill rotation, and wagyu galbi cut to 5mm — the thickness that maximizes caramelization surface area while preserving internal fat melt.

What the Top Score Reflects

The operation scoring 92 is a second-floor restaurant on Wilshire that does not participate in social media marketing and has no line management system — you either have a reservation or you don't eat. The galbi is sourced from a single Japanese Wagyu supplier, cut to 5mm, and marinated in a pear-soy-sesame blend for 24 hours. The charcoal is Japanese binchotan. The grill management is done by a table-side cook who does not leave your table during the meat service.

Jackson Heights is the most gastronomically diverse neighborhood in the United States, a fact that food journalists repeat as a truism but rarely operationalize. What it means practically is that Mexican restaurants in Jackson Heights serve a customer base that includes Mexican families who are eating the food they grew up with, and who will stop returning immediately if the quality doesn't meet their standard. That pressure is reflected in the scores.

The Tortilla at 95

The taqueria that produced our top score in the Jackson Heights dataset uses handmade corn tortillas pressed from masa prepared in-house from dried maize — not masa harina. The process takes 3 additional hours of daily prep versus using pre-made or masa harina versions. The result is a tortilla with a corn flavor profile and a texture that the pre-made alternatives cannot replicate. Our model has a tortilla quality dimension that scores freshness, corn flavor depth, and structural integrity. This kitchen scores 95.

"The tortilla is the chassis. A beautiful taco filling on a mediocre tortilla is like a great engine in a bad car — the limitation becomes the experience."

The carne asada is skirt steak, marinated in citrus and chile, grilled over charcoal. The al pastor is trompo-rotated, which is the correct technique. The chorizo is house-made with two chile varieties. The salsa verde and salsa roja are both made fresh twice daily. Value index: 97 — three tacos for $11 on weeknights. This is the correct taqueria in New York City.

There is a moment in Korean BBQ, approximately 4 minutes into cooking galbi over properly prepared binchotan charcoal, where the fat from the marbled short rib begins to render and fall onto the coals, producing a controlled flame that chars the exterior of the meat in a way no other heat source can replicate. That moment — the controlled char, the smoke, the caramelization of the marinade's sugar component — is the defining quality event of the form. The table at 92 points does this better than any other table in our LA dataset.

The 92 Point Table

The operation on Wilshire that tops our LA Korean BBQ dataset has been serving wagyu-specification galbi for six years. The kitchen uses binchotan — real Japanese charcoal, not pressed briquettes — managed by a dedicated table attendant who monitors coal temperature and rotation without interrupting conversation. The galbi is butterflied Prime short rib, 5mm thick, marinated in a pear-soy base for 24 hours minimum.

The banchan set — 9 items at last visit — is prepared in-house. The kimchi is house-fermented, 3 weeks minimum. The pajeon is cooked to order. The kongnamul is dressed with sesame oil and a trace of gochugaru rather than the typical bland soy-only version most operations serve. When every component of the meal is operating at this level simultaneously, the composite score becomes a given rather than a surprise.

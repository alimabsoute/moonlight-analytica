The 98 on Sourcing

Fish sourcing transparency is one of our six scoring dimensions, and it measures the verifiability and quality of the supply chain. The Little Tokyo counter uses three sourcing channels: a Tsukiji-affiliated importer operating out of the San Gabriel Valley, a dayboat operation out of Santa Barbara for local Pacific species, and a Japanese wild seafood specialist in Culver City for premium items like uni and live scallop. Every fish on the menu on any given night is traceable to a specific boat and date of catch.

The Rice at 96

The chef uses a blend of two Niigata-region Koshihikari varieties, seasoned with a custom red-white vinegar blend he has been refining for nine years. The ratio changes by season — heavier sugar in winter to balance the richer fish flavor, lighter and more acidic in summer. The rice is formed at 36°C and served within 35 seconds. Across six documented visits, the temperature variance was within ±1°C. That is the type of precision that produces a score of 96 on rice calibration and, ultimately, 96 overall.

# Tastyr IQ — Pitch Deck Notes & Talking Points

## The One-Liner
"Tastyr IQ rates **dishes**, not restaurants — AI-powered dish intelligence across 9 food verticals."

## The Problem
- **$371B** US restaurant industry relies on restaurant-level ratings (Yelp, Google)
- Nobody rates the actual FOOD — a 4-star restaurant can have 2-star dishes
- Consumers waste money on bad dishes at good restaurants
- Restaurants don't know which dishes are actually loved vs. tolerated
- No data layer exists at the dish level

## The Solution
- AI-powered platform that scores individual dishes on a 0-100 scale
- Aggregates reviews, social mentions, critic ratings, and proprietary signals
- 9 live verticals covering major cuisine categories
- Patent-pending scoring algorithm

## Key Objection Handling

**"Isn't this just another Yelp?"**
→ Yelp rates restaurants. We rate dishes. Think of it like this: Rotten Tomatoes rates movies, not theaters. We're Rotten Tomatoes for food.

**"How do you get dish-level data?"**
→ AI aggregation from multiple sources: menu data, review NLP extraction, social media mentions, critic reviews, and our proprietary scoring model. Patent pending.

**"What about data accuracy?"**
→ Our 9-vertical framework provides structured scoring, not just sentiment. Each dish gets a composite score from multiple signals, reducing noise.

**"Why not just add this to Yelp/Google?"**
→ Yelp/Google are restaurant-first platforms. Retrofitting dish intelligence into a restaurant platform is like adding movie ratings to a theater finder. The UX, data model, and use case are fundamentally different.

**"How do you make money?"**
→ Three revenue streams: consumer subscription, restaurant analytics dashboard (SaaS), and data licensing to delivery/CPG. Restaurant analytics alone is a $2B+ market.

**"Can't a big player just copy this?"**
→ First-mover data moat + patent pending. By the time someone copies, we'll have 50K+ dishes indexed with historical scoring data they can't replicate. Plus, our vertical-specific AI models improve with scale.

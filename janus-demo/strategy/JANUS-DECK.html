The problem

Physical spaces are operated blind.

Operators run multi-million-dollar floors on gut feel. The cameras are everywhere. The intelligence is nowhere.

01

Pixels lie.

Flat 2D zone polygons drawn on a video frame break the moment the camera moves an inch. A "zone" measured in pixels has no relationship to a foot of real floor — so the numbers can't compound, can't benchmark, can't survive a lens swap.

Polygon-on-frame model
02

POS is half the story.

Toast and Square tell you what was sold. They can't tell you what almost happened — the 40% of customers who walked in, hit the host stand, waited 6 minutes, and walked back out. That gap is where 60–80% of operational decisions live.

Transactions ≠ behavior
03

Surveillance ≠ intelligence.

CCTV records. It does not measure. Milestone, Genetec, and the next-tier VMS stack give you a video archive — not a dwell time, not a queue length, not a turn rate. Every operator on the planet still staffs on intuition.

Footage, not signal
Existing tools optimize for the screen.
Janus optimizes for the floor.

The breakthrough

Zones are surfaces, not pixels.

Janus zones are flat planes living in 3D world space, anchored to physical surfaces in real meters. Every count is computed against a measured floor — not a pixel area on a 2D frame.

Bar top at 1.05m. Dining table at 0.74m. Service counter at 0.91m. Floor at 0.00m.

01 Define

Name + size

Type, label, dimensions in meters.

02 Orient

Pitch · yaw · roll

Rotate the plane in 3D — TransformControls.

03 Anchor

Drop on surface

Translate to the bar top, table, ramp, dock.

04 Commit

Fill, label, live

Project through homography. Counts begin.

Move the camera, the zone stays. Swap the lens, the zone stays. Recalibrate once — zones survive forever.

Timing

Four tailwinds. One window.

The full stack — privacy law, open weights, edge silicon, defense procurement — moved into alignment in the last 18 months. Janus is one of the few companies architecturally configured to ride all four.

01

Edge compute is cheap.

Intel Iris Xe, Apple M-series, and Jetson Orin all run RF-DETR-Nano in real time. Cloud GPUs are no longer required for accurate detection — and that means no per-stream cloud cost line in the customer's deal.

02

Open detection weights.

RF-DETR is Apache 2.0 (replaces AGPL ultralytics). ByteTrack is MIT. Supervision is MIT. The whole detection / tracking / zone stack is permissive — no licensing trap on commercial deployment.

03

Facial recognition is off the table.

San Francisco, Boston, Portland banned it. The EU AI Act restricts it. CDAO direction moves DoD away from it. Janus's no-FR architecture isn't a limitation — it's the only thing left standing in healthcare, defense, and civic.

04

Defense modernization push.

SBIR funding is at all-time highs. AFWERX, DIU, SOFWERX onboard commercial tech faster than any time in the last 20 years. The window for an architecturally compliant CV stack to enter is open right now.

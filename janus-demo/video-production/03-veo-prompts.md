# Veo 3.1 B-Roll Prompts — Day 2

## Instructions
1. Open Google Flow / Veo 3.1 in AI Studio
2. Copy-paste each prompt exactly (style anchors are baked in)
3. Generate each clip — all are under 10 seconds (well within 3-min limit)
4. Review and regenerate if needed (aim for 2 takes per clip)
5. Download at highest resolution available (ideally 1080p or 4K)
6. All 8 clips can be generated in parallel

## Style Anchors (Already Included in Each Prompt)
Every prompt includes these quality anchors:
- `cinematic 24fps` — Film-look motion
- `shallow depth of field` — Professional bokeh
- Specified camera angle (medium/wide/close-up)
- `modern retail/office interior` or contextual setting
- No identifiable brands, logos, or recognizable faces

## Clip B1 — Frustrated Store Owner

**For Video**: The Problem (Video 2)
**Duration**: 5 seconds
**Used at**: 0:00-0:05
**Purpose**: Opening shot — establishes the pain of not having data

### Veo 3.1 Prompt:
```
Cinematic medium shot of a retail store owner standing behind a cash register, looking stressed and confused, warm interior retail lighting, shallow depth of field, modern clothing store with racks in background, 24fps, muted color palette, natural lighting from storefront windows
```

### Direction Notes:
- Subject should look perplexed, not angry
- Cash register area suggests "sales happened here but how many didn't?"
- Warm lighting creates empathy, not corporate coldness
- Clothing racks in background = recognizable retail environment
- Muted palette = the "before" state (vs. vibrant "after" with Janus)

## Clip B2 — Busy Store, No Data

**For Video**: The Problem (Video 2)
**Duration**: 5 seconds
**Used at**: 0:05-0:10
**Purpose**: Shows the chaos of a busy store with zero analytics

### Veo 3.1 Prompt:
```
Wide overhead shot of a busy modern retail store, 20-30 people walking through aisles, warm ambient lighting, slightly desaturated, cinematic 24fps, camera slowly dollying forward, clean modern shelving
```

### Direction Notes:
- Overhead angle mimics a security camera — establishing the "watching but not knowing" theme
- 20-30 people creates a "this is impossible to count" feeling
- Slow dolly forward adds cinematic motion
- Desaturated color = "raw footage without intelligence"
- No one person should be the focus — it's the crowd

**For Video**: Privacy-First (Video 4)
**Duration**: 8 seconds
**Used at**: 0:00-0:08 (left side of split-screen)
**Purpose**: Represents "the old way" — invasive surveillance

### Veo 3.1 Prompt:
```
Close-up of industrial CCTV surveillance camera with glowing red LED, mounted on concrete wall, harsh cold fluorescent lighting, desaturated dystopian color grade, ominous, 24fps
```

## Post-Generation Checklist

For each clip, verify:
- [ ] Duration matches spec (within ±1 second)
- [ ] No identifiable brands, logos, or text visible
- [ ] No recognizable real faces (AI-generated faces are fine)
- [ ] Color palette matches intended mood
- [ ] Camera motion is smooth (no jarring movements)
- [ ] Resolution is at least 1080p
- [ ] No watermarks or generation artifacts
- [ ] Clip has clean in/out points (no awkward starts/ends)
